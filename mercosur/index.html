A Puzzle: Why Does Mercosur Stay Together?

Since 1995, Mercosur has kept a common external tariff and an internal free-trade area. Uruguay threatens exit, external negotiations stall, yet the bloc persists.

What we see

  • → Uruguay floats China/USA bilateral deals
  • → External talks drag for years
  • → The bloc never actually breaks

Why it matters

  • → Shapes supply chains for 280M people
  • → Stability parallels other regional blocs
  • → Sector shocks drive political pressure
Key insight: Small members gain bargaining power in the bloc; large members face concentrated manufacturing losses, so they stall external deals.

Why Large Countries Block Deals

Brazil/Argentina see small aggregate gains but concentrated sector losses → political pushback.

Losses are concentrated (apparel, textiles, leather) and politically vocal. Gains are diffuse (consumers, agriculture). Result: vetoes and delays.
